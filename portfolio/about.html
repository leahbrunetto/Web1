I am an artist, designer, and scholar living in Cambridge, MA. My visual concepts are inspired by diverse subject matter including dreams, the animal kingdom, natural phenomena, the built world, human-nature interactions, and intra- and interpersonal dynamics. These subject areas are embedded within allegories and conceptual abstractions in a manner reminiscent of fables or myths. In my work, I study the universe on all orders of magnitude—comparing events such as volcanic eruptions and whale spouts and connecting cavities such as apple bites and black holes—as an exploration of space, feeling, and meaning.

I will graduate this spring with a Bachelor of Science in Architecture from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, as well as additional experience in computer science, strobe laboratory, astronomy, and brain + cognitive sciences. After graduation, I plan to continue producing and exhibiting projects and pursue opportunities in contemporary art, web design, and other areas which interest me such as scientific illustration, landscape design, and design for animal welfare.

This website highlights a selection of recent work which exists across media and academic disciplines. I will be uploading older work over the next couple months. An archive of old work from 2004-2009 can be found at brunetto.mit.edu.
