Air Pollution Group

Fall 2010

For a semester long workshop, my group developed a series of performative objects which responded to the issue of air pollution. In this “responsive bodywear”, lavender (adhered by honey to a rice paper surface) lines up with the bottom of the nose to mask unpleasant smells of car exhaust, and often the objects acted as filters. Important to the development of these designs was that all of the materials were biodegradable. We brought our lavender airplane masks--one of several prototypes-- into the public realm in a documented performance. They were mass produced in studio and thrown out over Memorial Drive (Cambridge, MA) as an act symbolic of cleaning air. In this performance, I imagine myself as a mythic character who grows lavender and flies it across the polluted cityscape using biodegradable materials as a substrate. The airplanes can caught by humans and then inverted and fitted over the face, or might instead engage the environment. As an airplane hits the ground, it can be imagined to sink through the Earth over time, planting seeds into the ground to grow new lavender plants (propogating a cycle).
