Leah Brunetto is an artist, designer scholar living in Cambridge, MA. Her visual concepts are inspired by childlike fantasies, the animal kingdom, natural processes and phenomena, human-nature interactions, science and engineering, religion and classical mythology, states of consciousness, intra- and interpersonal dynamics, and her cultural heritage. These subject areas are embedded within allegories and conceptual abstractions in a manner often reminiscent of fables or myths. In her work, Leah aims to study the universe on all orders of magnitude—comparing events such as volcanic eruptions and whale spouts and connecting cavities such as apple bites and black holes—as an exploration of space, feeling, and meaning. Leah will graduate this spring with a Bachelor of Science in Architecture from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
