I am an artist, designer, and scholar living in Cambridge, MA. My visual concepts are inspired by diverse subject matter including childlike fantasies, the animal kingdom, natural phenomena, architecture, human-nature interactions, science and engineering, religion and classical mythology, states of consciousness, intra- and interpersonal dynamics, and my cultural heritage. These subject areas are embedded within allegories and conceptual abstractions in a manner reminiscent of fables or myths. In my work, I study the universe on all orders of magnitude—comparing events such as volcanic eruptions and whale spouts and connecting cavities such as apple bites and black holes—as an exploration of space, feeling, and meaning. I will graduate this spring with a Bachelor of Science in Architecture from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, with a tailored study in the Art, Culture, and Technology Program (School of Architecture + Planning) as well as many additional science electives.

This website highlights a selection of recent work which exists across media and academic disciplines. I will be uploading older work over the next couple months. For more info, please e-mail me at brunetto at mit dot edu.
