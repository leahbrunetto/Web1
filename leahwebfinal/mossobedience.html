Moss Obedience

Spring 2011

I am interested in fusions of the human body with the natural world both physically and symbolically.Moss Obedience is a visualization of how an obedient nature can develop over time to encompass the self and blend with physical and psychological environments. The mossy “mask” leaves up to interpretation whether the moss is merged/emanating from the self, or whether it is simply a prop. I chose a dog as the universal symbol of loyalty, and a poodle specifically because it a hypoallergenic breed (I have allergies) and aesthetically distinctive.With the same efforts as water color painting, I finished the mask on site by using loose moss and pine needles to fill in gaps and “blend”. The accompanying self portraits were shot in the backyard of my parents’ Cape house in Dennisport, MA; the next door neighbors have standard poodles.
