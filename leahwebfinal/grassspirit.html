Grass Spirit

Fall 2011

The process of creation started by painting my feet (in the color blue, in the style of Yves Klein) and then walking over a blank sheet of paper. After the paint dried, I traced the painted prints over notebook paper and used those templates for cutting astroturf. The design concept is rooted in the idea of site as ecosystem. The areas of the ground that came in contact with the foot are imagined to have been made fertile, resulting in the growth of grass. This is made counterintuitive in context of the site of installation, a gymnasium, but addressed comically with the use of astroturf, the most athletic of grasses. The idea of the "grass spirit" is in the start and finish of the path of footprints: the first and last few only show part of the foot on the ground, imagining that the traveler came down from high in the air and right back up again; the path has no defined start or end on the plane of the ground. .
